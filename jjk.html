Jujustu Kaisen is gaining lot of popularity this fall 2023

Reasons why you should watch jjk
Jujutsu Kaisen follows the story of Yuji Itadori, a regular high school student who finds himself in the middle of the secret world of Jujutsu sorcerers after he eats the severed finger of a demonic entity called Sukuna which grants him powers of a "Curse". The anime from Studio MAPPA came out in October 2020 and ran for 24 episodes till March 2021. It has again became the talk of the town after the release of its prequel movie Jujutsu Kaisen 0 in theatres around the world. Based on the manga by Gege Akutami, the anime was awarded “Anime of the Year” by Crunchyroll, and quickly became one of the top shonen anime series of all time according to anime fans. Here are five reasons why you should be watching Jujutsu Kaisen right now.

| The Story’s Execution Is Flawless
No pacing issues here. Jujutsu Kaisen introduces its large number of characters, establishes the Jujutsu world, and explains the power system without it feeling abrupt and out of place. Every element is given enough time, characters are fleshed out nicely, and even the story is set up smoothly for future seasons.

| Both Male and Female Characters Are Written Well
While Satoru Gojou, Yuji Itadori, and Megumi Fushigoro are obviously fleshed out given they’re the main male characters in a shonen anime—the show gets female characters like Nobara Kugisaki, Maki and Mai Zenin right too—making them some much-needed competent female characters.

| Jujutsu Kaisen Can Go From Lighthearted to Dark Quickly
The show will make sure to get belly laughs out of you as it finds the right balance between action and comedy. Yuji is a hilarious protagonist whose antics never let him get stale as a shonen main character. However, Jujutsu Kaisen isn’t afraid to go dark either. The world has a horror feel to it as it is full of monsters and supernatural entities.

| The Animation Will Leave You Wanting More
Produced by Studio MAPPA which worked on the latter seasons of Attack on Titan, Jujutsu Kaisen offers fluid motions in fight scenes and smooth animations even when you would normally expect a studio to cut corners for a TV show. The show doesn’t hold back even on character expressions, making them feel alive.
